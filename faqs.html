F.A.Q

1What does it cost to bring siblings to STEM?

The first child that enroll in STEM class pays the full price for the season. Siblings enrolled at the same time receive a 30% discount off the cost of the season. If an enrolled explorer's sibling is 12 months old or younger and will not fully participate in class, that child is welcome to come at no cost and does not need to enroll.
If you have previously enrolled one child and would later like to enroll a second child in the same class, please email us at support@stem.com so we can help apply the discount to these separate enrollments.

2 What are STEM classes like?

STEM classes take place in public green spaces and engage children in activities that foster a sense of joy and wonder, all while developing important skills. Each class follows a loose structure that includes an opening circle, main activity, closing circle and snack. The main activity focuses on open-ended exploration and play where kids actively engage in imagining, exploring and/or problem solving. While this activity is planned and guided by an adult, the interests of the children play a large role in shaping the way it unfolds. Snack time then allows children and their adults to relax and reflect on their learning and experiences.

3What do STEM classes teach?

STEM lessons are engineered to help kids develop a range of essential physical, cognitive and social-emotional skills. These skills support kids at being ready to learn, ready to thrive and ready for anything. We make learning irresistible by designing each experience to inspire wonder, activate the senses, leverage brain science, draw on wisdom of the ages and unleash the talents of our passionate network of educators, parents and caregivers.

4 What happens if I miss a day of class?

At STEM, we deeply value the learning community that develops within each class over the course of a season, so we encourage families to plan to attend every week. For this reason, we do not prorate class costs, or offer future season credits/refunds based on missed sessions.
However, we know that things can come up which may impact your ability to make it to class from time to time. If you are away, kids are sick, or the planets simply don't align one day and you miss a STEM class, we welcome you to make up that session. Families can attend up to two sessions per season as make-ups, and they can attend these make-ups either in another class running within that season, in the next season that their leader offers, or with another local leader.
Makeup sessions are subject to availability. In order to request a makeup session, please reach out directly to the leader whose class you'd like to attend to confirm whether they can accommodate you and your child.

5 How old are the children who attend STEM classes?

STEM classes are available to children from 18 months to 8 years of age. Some classes focus on a specific age range (such as 18-36 months, 3-5 years, etc). Others are available to children anywhere between 18 months and 8 years. By working with a smaller age range, classes can be more finely tailored to the developmental level of the children who participate, while allowing a larger age range lets whole families participate together. The larger age range provides a chance for younger kids to benefit from the modeling and support of older kids, and for older kids to be leaders for their younger classmates. In general, age ranges are meant to be used as guidelines, and many activities can be adapted for younger or older children. However, if your child is outside of the recommended age range for a particular class, it's a good idea to reach out to the leader of that class before enrolling to ensure that your child has the skills he or she will need to participate in and enjoy the planned activities. To search for classes by age, please visit www.STEM.com/classes and use the drop-down menu to select the age range that best fits your children.

6 Are classes designed to allow parents to drop off their children?

One of the best parts of the STEM experience is that classes help parents and caregivers to develop new ways of engaging, exploring and learning with their children. When parents and caregivers participate in classes, they gain valuable insights; often, they come away surprised by just what their children are capable of doing, and how much problem solving and skill building occurs when kids have the opportunity to play outside. Because of the important benefits that adults and children enjoy when they share in this experience, STEM classes are not designed to allow children to be dropped off.

7 Can I try out a class before enrolling?

Yes! Prior to the start of a class, leaders will often organize free class sessions so that parents and children can experience a "Taste of STEM". These free events, and all classes, can be found by visiting www.STEM.com/classes and using the drop-down menu to select your location. Once you've found a free event that you'd like to attend, you can register from there. In addition, leaders are often happy to have a family join for a trial session even after classes have commenced. If you are interested in joining for a particular session, please reach out to the leader of that class by email. You can click to email a leader from the description page for each class.
